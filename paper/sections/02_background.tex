\subsection{Pediatric Unintentional Injury Prevention}
According to the World Health Organization \cite{who2008world}, approximately 90\% of injuries to young children occur in or around their home. Falls, particularly stair falls, are a leading cause of pediatric emergency department visits \cite{ali2019consumer}.

\subsection{Systems Safety and Human Factors}
Systems-theoretic approaches to safety, such as STAMP \cite{leveson2011engineering}, emphasize the role of control structures and constraints rather than merely component failures. Human trust in automation \cite{lee2004trust} and automation bias \cite{parasuraman1997humans} complicate the introduction of AI into safety-critical domains.

\subsection{Formal Verification and Proof-Carrying Systems}
Model checking \cite{baier2008principles} provides algorithmic verification of finite-state systems. Proof-carrying code \cite{necula1997proof} established the paradigm where an untrusted producer provides a machine-checkable proof of safety. We extend this to proof-carrying recommendations for physical environments.

\subsection{LLM-Assisted Theorem Proving}
Recent advances have integrated LLMs with interactive theorem provers like Lean 4 \cite{moura2021lean}. Systems such as LeanDojo \cite{yang2023leandojo} enable programmatic interaction and retrieval-augmented proving. However, most existing work focuses on formalising pure mathematics rather than cyber-physical or domestic safety scenarios.
